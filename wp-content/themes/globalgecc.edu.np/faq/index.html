Q: How do I Choose a Course?

A: Choosing a course is a matter of research and analysis. Many Colleges/Universities may offer a course in your chosen subject. You have to chart your geographical, climatic and location preference and analyze apart from other things, the duration of the course, the fees, the living cost in that city, the faculty, the facilities and the content of the course.

Q: What is the duration of Course?

A: Courses vary in their time schedules. Diploma courses are shorter that degree programs. Masters, usually are about two years in duration. Doctorate programs require longer time inputs. Similarly short term certificate courses can be from a few weeks to a couple of months. Please consult individual programs brochures for more detail on the duration of specific course.

Q: Is the course internationally recognized?

A: All international Students intake colleges are internationally recognized. However, this is may vary from country to country and from the subject to subject. Many countries have mandatory regulations within their education system, which need to be compiled with, and therefore, a thorough check must be made before you opt for a particular course.

Q: What is the approximate cost of study and cost of stay Abroad?

A: The tuition fee depends upon the duration and type of course. Please refer to specific program brochures of the universities to get an idea of the exact cost. The cost of food and accommodation varies marginally from city to city and again the university, which you intend to select for your study, is in the best position to offer you correct advice on this matter.

Q: Will I be allowed to travel to other parts of the country?

A: There are no restrictions on students travelling around the country. Provided the course offers you the spare time, you can visit places of your choice and enjoy the experience of different people, culture and lands.

Q: Are there any students from Nepal studying in the countries, You Desire to Study?

A: Ay any given time, Thousands of students from all over the world are studying in every country as such worldwide. Yes, there are students from Nepal as well. However, since many countries are very big geographically and universities are spread far and wide, you will find someone from Nepal in your chosen university or chosen city. If not, at least in and around area’s Colleges and Universities.

Q: I do not understand good English, how do I overcome this problem?

A: English is widely spoken and it’s the International Language, and it is spoken everywhere in most of the countries. so, it is a great advantage if you are able to become more fluent in the use of the language. Also there are various test been conducted to test the level of English Fluency of the students to allow the entry to various University/Colleges abroad except India. However, you should not be demotivated as such one can get fluency after attending various classes through the specialized coaching centres.

Q: Where do I stay and what kind of food can I expect to get?

A: Every College/University, which is the part of the International students program, has prior arrangements to host you. This may either be in an existing hostel, or off campus in residential accommodation hired for this purpose. Whatever is the case, you will be explained about the facilities and the procedures involved in getting accommodation before leaving your country. Every country offers wide variety of Asian, Western and Oriental Cuisine. Most international students find it easy to adjust to food conditions and enjoy every flavour, aromas and the tastes that country offers.

Q: What are the labs and hospital practice like?
A: The universities have their own first-class medical labs with good facilities in the nationwide. And all the Universities have comprehensive affiliated teaching and practicing hospitals approved by Ministry of Education and Ministry of Healh.

Q: How long will it take to get my VISA?
A: After you receive your School admission letter or confirmation letter, time is varied by the country. for example, to study in India you don’t need any kind of Visa. To study in Bangladesh it takes about 1 week for VISA. For CANADA, USA, AUSTRALIA it varies as of the Embassy of respective country says.

Q: How do I get admission into program?
A: First of all you must be eligible for course you are applying and must be affiliated by the Government bodies of the respective countries. Once you are eligible, one can get admission in desired college through us.
